### **Research Summary & Interests**

I am a recent Ph.D. graduate in computational chemistry, with a core focus on **catalytic reaction mechanisms** and **machine learning interatomic potentials**. During my Ph.D., I developed neural network potentials for transition-metal oxide surfaces, enabling high-throughput catalyst screening. My research bridges established first-principles methods with data-driven approaches, focusing on:

1. **Neural Network Potentials for Catalysis**: Extending ML-based force fields to capture catalytic reaction pathways at near-DFT accuracy with 100× speedup.
2. **High-Throughput Catalyst Discovery**: Combining DFT descriptors with ML models to screen candidate materials for electrochemical CO₂ reduction.
3. **Metal-Organic Framework Design**: Rational design of MOFs for gas capture and separation via computational screening.
